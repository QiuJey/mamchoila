Artificial intelligence — or AI — is the ability of a computer system to perform tasks that would normally require human thinking. That includes things like recognizing your face in a photo, understanding what you say to a voice assistant, or suggesting the next show you might want to watch. At its core, AI is about teaching machines to observe patterns, make decisions, and improve over time — much like we do, but at a much faster scale.

## How AI Works

AI systems learn by processing large amounts of data and finding patterns within it. Rather than being manually programmed with every possible rule, an AI model is trained on examples — and from those examples it builds its own internal understanding of how to respond to new situations.

Here are a few real-world examples of AI in action:

- **Recommendation engines** — When Netflix suggests a film or Spotify builds you a playlist, an AI has analyzed your past behavior and compared it to millions of other users to predict what you'll enjoy next.
- **Voice assistants** — When you ask Siri or Alexa a question, AI converts your spoken words into text, figures out what you mean, and finds an appropriate response — all in under a second.
- **Image recognition** — When your phone unlocks using your face, or Google Photos groups pictures of the same person together, AI is identifying visual patterns to match and categorize what it sees.

## Where You See AI Every Day

AI isn't something that only exists in science labs or tech companies — it's woven into the tools and services you already use:

- **Email** — Spam filters automatically sort unwanted messages out of your inbox.
- **Search engines** — Google ranks results based on what it predicts you're actually looking for.
- **Online shopping** — Product recommendations appear based on your browsing and purchase history.
- **Maps and navigation** — Apps like Google Maps predict traffic and reroute you in real time.
- **Social media** — Your feed is curated by an algorithm that learns what content keeps you engaged.
- **Banking** — Fraud detection systems flag unusual transactions by spotting patterns that don't match your normal behavior.
