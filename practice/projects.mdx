Projects are where learning becomes doing. At AI Academy, every project gives you a chance to take the concepts you've studied and put them to work in a real context — whether that means experimenting with a new AI tool, building something from scratch, or collaborating with fellow learners. Completing projects deepens your understanding far beyond what reading or watching alone can achieve, and they form a core part of your path to certification.

## Types of Projects

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Guided Projects" icon="map">
    Follow a step-by-step walkthrough that leads you through every stage of a project. Guided projects are ideal when you're just getting started and want structure and support as you build.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Open Projects" icon="lightbulb">
    Apply your own ideas to an open-ended brief. Open projects give you creative freedom to explore a topic in a direction that interests you, with no single right answer.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Course Projects" icon="graduation-cap">
    Complete a required project tied directly to your course curriculum. Course projects are assessed and must be submitted to earn your course certificate.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Community Projects" icon="people-group">
    Team up with other AI Academy learners to tackle a shared challenge. Community projects help you practice collaboration and learn from the approaches others bring.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Featured Beginner Projects

Get started with one of these beginner-friendly projects, each designed to build practical skills you can use right away.

<Accordion title="Build a Chatbot Prompt Library">
  Design and organise a personal library of reusable prompts for a conversational AI tool. You'll test different prompt structures, compare the outputs you get, and document what works best for specific use cases like writing, brainstorming, or summarising.

  **Skills you'll practise:**
  - Writing clear, effective prompts
  - Iterating on prompts to improve AI responses
  - Organising and documenting your work
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Classify Images with an AI Tool">
  Use an AI-powered image recognition tool to categorise a set of sample images by subject, mood, or content type. You'll explore how the tool interprets visual input and learn to evaluate and refine its classifications.

  **Skills you'll practise:**
  - Working with AI vision tools
  - Evaluating and critiquing AI output
  - Understanding classification and labelling concepts
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Write an AI-Powered Blog Post">
  Collaborate with an AI writing assistant to plan, draft, and edit a short blog post on an AI-related topic of your choice. The goal is to use AI as a creative partner while keeping your own voice and judgement front and centre.

  **Skills you'll practise:**
  - Using AI writing tools effectively
  - Editing and refining AI-generated content
  - Balancing AI assistance with human oversight
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Create an AI Image Gallery">
  Generate a themed collection of images using an AI image creation tool. You'll write descriptive prompts, experiment with different styles, and curate your best results into a short gallery with captions explaining your prompt choices.

  **Skills you'll practise:**
  - Writing descriptive image prompts
  - Exploring AI image generation tools
  - Curating and presenting AI-created content
</Accordion>

## How to Submit a Project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete your course">
    Work through all the lessons and modules in your course before starting the associated project. The concepts you've covered will directly inform your work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the project section">
    Navigate to the **Projects** tab within your course dashboard. You'll find the project brief, any supporting resources, and the submission form all in one place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow the brief">
    Read the project brief carefully. It outlines what you need to deliver, any constraints to keep in mind, and the criteria your submission will be assessed against.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit your work">
    Upload your completed project using the submission form. Depending on the project type, you may submit a written document, a file, a link, or a combination of these.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get feedback">
    Once your submission is reviewed, you'll receive written feedback in your dashboard. Use that feedback to understand your strengths and identify areas to keep developing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Course projects are **required** for certification. You must submit and pass the course project before you can request your certificate. Ready to earn yours? Head to [Certificates](/practice/certificates) to see what's available.
</Note>
