## What You'll Learn

Work through five modules that build on each other, taking you from first principles all the way to responsible AI use.

<Accordion title="Module 1: What Is AI?">
  Discover what artificial intelligence really means and where it came from. You'll explore the history of AI, debunk common myths, and understand the difference between narrow AI and general AI. By the end of this module, you'll be able to explain AI in plain terms to anyone.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Module 2: Machine Learning Basics">
  Learn how machines learn from data rather than being explicitly programmed. You'll cover supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning — with everyday examples that make each concept click. No math required.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Module 3: Deep Learning Overview">
  Go one level deeper and find out how neural networks — the technology behind voice assistants, image recognition, and large language models — actually work. You'll understand the key ideas without needing to write a single line of code.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Module 4: AI in the Real World">
  See where AI is being used right now — in healthcare, finance, education, creative industries, and more. You'll analyze real examples of AI at work and think critically about the benefits and limitations of each application.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Module 5: Ethics & Responsible AI">
  Explore the important questions that come with powerful technology: bias, fairness, privacy, and accountability. You'll learn how to think critically about AI systems and understand what responsible AI development looks like.
</Accordion>

## By the End of This Course

After completing AI Fundamentals, you will be able to:

- Explain what AI is and how it differs from traditional software
- Describe how machine learning and deep learning work at a conceptual level
- Identify real-world applications of AI across multiple industries
- Recognize common limitations and failure modes of AI systems
- Discuss ethical considerations around bias, fairness, and privacy in AI
- Speak confidently about AI in professional or everyday conversations
